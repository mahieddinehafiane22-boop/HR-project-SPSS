# -HR-project-hypothesis-testing-SPSS
A project to test if the quality of life  impacts the overall well being of employees at NAFTAL using real world data gathered by distributing surveys to a sample of 40 employees from a population of 131 personnels. 
THIS PROJECT WAS CONDUCTED AS A PART OF AN INTERNSHIP PROGRAM.
The full analysis/interpretations can be found on the PDF file along with the SPSS results.
OVERVIEW : This project analyzes the impact of Quality of Working Life (QWL) on employee well-being using a quantitative case study conducted at NAFTAL GPL,Data were collected from 40 employees through a structured questionnaire and analyzed using statistical methods to assess the relationship between working conditions and employee well-being.
METHODOLOGY : Quantitative survey-based research.
Sample size: 40 employees.
reliability testing using Cronbach’s Alpha.
Analysis techniques:descriptive statistics, Correlation analysis, Simple linear regression.
KEY FINDINGS : The study confirms a significant positive relationship between Quality of Working Life and employee well-being.
MAIN INSIGHTS:
Social and professional relationships improve social well-being.
Job content and work organization enhance subjective well-being.
Physical work environment impacts physical well-being.
Professional development and work–life balance increase overall employee satisfaction.
IMPACT : The findings position QWL as a strategic lever for: Improving employee well-being, Enhancing organizational health, Supporting talent retention and performance.
